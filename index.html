Understand The Difference Between Your
"Range Swing" And Your "Real Swing"

Some driving ranges now offer videotaping services. Practice on the range is important for developing muscle memory, but most golfers who crush it on the driving range are frustrated to find they cannot repeat that performance out on the course. This is because the conditions on the driving range are totally different. Think about it . . .

On the range you are:

  • Hitting 20 balls in a row with the same club from a perfect lie; and
  • There's no pressure to perform. If you hit a clunker, who cares?

Whereas on the course you have to:

  • Switch clubs on each shot;
  • Hit from the rough, bunkers, and uneven lies; and
  • Deal with the pressure of scoring well.
